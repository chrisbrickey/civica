# Acquiring French Nationality
Content was copied from this website on 2026-07-10: https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/devenir-francais/procedures-dacces-a-nationalite-francaise

## Procedure Selection
Since January 1, 2026:
- The applicant must demonstrate knowledge of the French language in writing and speaking at level B2 of the CEFR, for applications for naturalization and reinstatement, as well as for marriage declaration procedures;
- The applicant must provide proof of passing the civic examination, both for naturalization and for reinstatement in French nationality.

The main procedures for acquiring French nationality that fall under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of the Interior are:
1) FOLLOW THIS / COPIED BELOW: Procedures by decree (naturalization and reintegration)
2) IGNORE THIS / NOT COPIED: the declaratory procedures (marriage, ascendants, brothers and sisters of French citizens)

## Naturalization and reintegration into French nationality by decree
Any foreigner with a valid residence permit has the right to apply for French nationality through the naturalization procedure by decree.
A foreigner who has previously been French has the right to apply to regain French nationality under certain conditions through the reinstatement procedure by decree.

### Preparation and filing of the application
The documents required to apply for or regain French nationality, regardless of the foreigner's situation, are as follows:
- 1 official identity document (passport, identity card)
- 1 tax stamp worth €255
- 1 double-sided copy of your valid residence permit (unless you are a national of a European state)

Depending on the foreign national's situation, other documents may be requested by the French government. To assist applicants, a simulator (https://www.service-public.gouv.fr/simulateur/calcul/Naturalisation) to obtain a personalized list of required documents is available on the website service-public.gouv.fr.

Applications for naturalization or reinstatement of French nationality by decree are submitted online on the website of the Digital Administration for Foreigners in France (https://administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr/usagers/#/).
_You cannot apply online if you reside in French Guiana, Saint Barthélemy, Saint Martin, Saint Pierre and Miquelon, Wallis and Futuna, French Polynesia, or New Caledonia. You must submit your paper application to the naturalization platform in your place of residence. If you live abroad, the application must be submitted to a French consulate._

The applicant must prepare their file, either by scanning or photographing all the required documents.

Users can get help from the Citizen Contact Centre (0 806 001 620, free call). For those having difficulty completing the online process, there are Digital Support Points at the prefecture to guide applicants through the procedure (by appointment only).

The French nationality access platform responsible for the applicant's place of residence examines the completeness of the applicant's file.

If the file is incomplete, she can:
- request additional information
- The case will be closed without further action if these additional documents have not been submitted within the deadlines.

### Demonstrate a level of proficiency in French

Since January 1, 2026, the applicant must demonstrate a knowledge of the French language, both spoken and written, at least equal to level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) .

He will need to provide a French diploma or proof of passing a certified or internationally recognized language test.

However, there are accommodations or even exemptions for taking the tests used to assess language level. Applicants may be granted accommodations or exemptions from certain parts of the French language assessment if their disability or health condition requires it. They must provide a medical certificate attesting that accommodations are necessary, specifying the recommended accommodations, or stating that their disability or health condition makes the language assessment impossible.

### Civic exam

To be naturalized as a French citizen, the applicant must demonstrate their assimilation into the French community, in particular through sufficient knowledge of French history, culture and society.

The expected level of knowledge is assessed during a civic exam, implemented from January 1, 2026, consisting of a multiple-choice questionnaire, written in French, of 40 questions including knowledge questions and situational questions.
A minimum of 32 correct answers (80%) is required to pass. The test is administered digitally and lasts a maximum of 45 minutes.

The questions cover the 5 themes of civic education:
1) Principles and values of the French Republic
2) Institutional and political system
3) Rights and responsibilities
4) History, geography and culture
5) Living in French society

To prepare for the exam, applicants can consult:
- the citizen's handbook (https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/documentation/guides-textes-et-brochures/livret-du-citoyen.html, downloaded)
- the charter of the rights and duties of French citizens (https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/documentation/guides-textes-et-brochures/charte-des-droits-et-devoirs-du-citoyen-francais.html; downloaded)
- the list of knowledge questions (https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/documentation/guides-textes-et-brochures/questions-de-connaissance-pour-lexamen-civique-nationalite-francaise.html; downloaded)
- The situational questions are not available.

#### Unofficial sources for situational questions
- Questions-naturalisation.fr: contains questions where you must choose what is the correct legal or civic behaviour in a situation (e.g. respect of law, role of the State, equality between women and men). 
- Letestcivique.fr and similar sites: provide “questions essentielles” and sometimes mini‑scenarios for the interview or civic test. 
- Franceprefecture.fr, Qcmcivique.fr, Examencivique-france.fr: offer large sets of questions and mock tests; some include questions phrased as situations, especially on “droits et devoirs” and daily life (work, school, secularism).

#### Passing the civic exam

Two organizations have been approved by the Ministry of the Interior to implement the civic examination:

- The Paris Chamber of Commerce and Industry (CCIP). Find approved centers and registration procedures on the CCIP website (https://francais.cci-paris-idf.fr/candidat?produit=21).
- France Éducation International (FEI):
  - Fill out the pre-registration form on the website test-civique.fr (https://test-civique.fr/inscription)
  - Find your nearest test center on the FEI website (https://www.france-education-international.fr/centres-d-examen/carte?type-centre=examen_civique)

However, there are accommodations or even exemptions for taking the tests to assess the level of knowledge of French history, culture and society.
The applicant may be granted accommodations or exemptions from certain tests for this assessment if their disability or health condition requires it. They must provide a medical certificate attesting that accommodations are necessary, specifying the recommended accommodations, or indicating that their disability or health condition makes the language assessment impossible.


### Interview at the prefecture
Once the application is deemed complete, the applicant is invited to an interview at the prefecture by the French nationality access platform. The interview is conducted by an agent appointed by the prefect and assesses the applicant's integration into French society.

During the interview, the applicant must present the original civil status documents submitted on the website of the Digital Administration for Foreigners in France or sent by mail.

The interview report and the documents in the file allow the prefect to decide on the next steps in the process.

To prepare for their interview, applicants can refer to the citizen's handbook and the charter of the rights and duties of the French citizen .


### Prefect's decision
At the end of the review of the application, the prefect may make an unfavorable decision (postponing, rejecting or declaring the application inadmissible) or propose naturalization. 

If naturalization is proposed by the prefect, the file is forwarded to the Ministry of the Interior, which assesses the application and may:
- validate the favorable proposal and prepare a naturalization decree, signed by the Prime Minister and published in the Official Journal 
- to make an unfavorable decision

After the applicant's name is registered in a decree granting them citizenship, the central civil registry office issues a birth certificate, which is required for the issuance of a national identity card or passport. 
Information on how to obtain a copy of this certificate can be found on the website of the Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs (https://www.diplomatie.gouv.fr/fr/services-aux-francaises-et-aux-francais/vie-administrative-et-elections/etat-civil-et-nationalite-francaise/etat-civil/demander-un-acte-d-etat-civil).


### Reception into French citizenship
A solemn and symbolic ceremony welcoming new citizens into French citizenship is organised in each department by the prefect in the presence of elected officials within 6 months of acquiring French nationality.
